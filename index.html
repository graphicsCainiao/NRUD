Various Degradation

Neural rendering performance depends on clear, high-resolution inputs. In real-world scenarios, however, images often suffer from degradations such as blur, uneven lighting, low resolution, adverse weather, and occlusions, which severely affect rendering quality and downstream tasks like 3D reconstruction and scene understanding. To enhance robustness in such conditions, many studies have aimed to reduce the impact of single or multiple degradations on rendering quality.

Methodological Analysis

This survey reviews neural rendering under degraded inputs and classifies existing studies into 7 representative tasks: Deblurring, Adverse Illumination Restoration, Super Resolution, Weather Restoration, Underwater Restoration, Post-enhancement and Others. We summarize the representative work and progress in each task.

We also provide an in-depth analysis of seven degradation tasks and their technical solutions

Future Work

We outline 5 promising research directions, including Dataset, Camera Pose Estimation, General Restoration Frameworks, Dynamic Scene Restoration, Efficiency.
Additionally, we highlight 5 application domains of neural rendering under degradations, such as autonomous driving, VR/AR, cultural heritage preservation, film production, and medical imaging visualization.
